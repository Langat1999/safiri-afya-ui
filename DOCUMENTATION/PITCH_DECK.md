# SLIDE 2: THE PROBLEM

## Kenya's Healthcare Accessibility Crisis

### üè• Limited Access to Healthcare
- **47 million** Kenyans, only **1 doctor per 16,000 people** (WHO recommendation: 1:1,000)
- **60% of population** lives in rural areas with minimal healthcare infrastructure
- Average distance to nearest health facility: **10+ kilometers** in rural areas

### üí∞ High Cost of Healthcare
- **47% of Kenyans** spend out-of-pocket for healthcare
- Average consultation fee: **KES 500-2,000** ($3.50-$14)
- Many avoid seeking care due to cost uncertainty

### üì± Digital Divide in Healthcare
- **88% smartphone penetration** but limited digital health solutions
- No centralized platform for clinic discovery and booking
- Patients waste time and money visiting wrong facilities

### üåç Language Barriers
- Most health information only available in English
- **67% of population** primarily speaks Swahili or local languages
- Health literacy gap affects treatment outcomes

**THE RESULT:** Delayed diagnoses, unnecessary emergency visits, and preventable deaths
